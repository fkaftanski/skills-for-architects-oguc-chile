# Normative Audit

## Scope

Repository-wide audit of normative classification, source hierarchy, and legal wording.

## Main findings

1. The repository previously mixed primary legal sources, interpretive circulars, technical references, and sectoral context without an explicit hierarchy.
2. DS 50 was exposed as a parking or energy-related concept in some surrounding wording; it must be treated as accessibility universal.
3. Electrical-installation references needed a working distinction between the historical NCh Elec. 4/2003 frame and the operational RIC / Decreto 08 frame.
4. The disclaimer language was too weak and used legally noisy references.
5. Several historical files represented a prompt library rather than a maintainable knowledge package.

## Updated classifications

- Primary legal: LGUC, OGUC, Ley 21.442, Ley 20.958, Ley 21.305, DS 40/2012, Ley 17.288.
- Official interpretive: DDU circulars.
- Official operational: DOM en Línea, Proveedores Técnicos MINVU.
- Official referential: IPT/IDE/maps/search tools.
- Technical reference: INN and technical norms.
- Sectoral secondary: gremial and professional associations.
- Excluded from core: non-traceable or opinionated sources.

## Normative gaps still requiring human validation

- Canonical URLs for every registry entry.
- Exact operational scope of each DDU circular used in a live response.
- Any municipal override for predial or communal queries.
- Exact regulatory crosswalks for specialty domains not yet expanded in skills.

## Recommended maintenance rule

Before any future normative answer:

1. Identify source category.
2. Prefer primary legal text.
3. Declare interpretive or referential use explicitly.
4. Contrast with local instrument when the query is predial or communal.
5. Defer when the authoritative source is not verified.
